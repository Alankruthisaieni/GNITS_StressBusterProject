Top 7 Stress Busting foods

1.Chamomile Herbal Tea

Chamomile is a medicinal herb that has been used since ancient times as a natural stress reducer.Author Stephanie Ross states that chamomile tea increases serotonin and melatonin in the body, two hormones that work to alleviate feelings of stress.By increasing these hormones, you can relax more easily and let the stress of the day slip away. For the best results using this herbal remedy, drink one to two cups of chamomile tea daily or when feelings particularly stressed out.

Chemical compounds and antioxidants in chamomile tea works to open up blood vessels and reduce inflammation that can constrict and lead to pounding headaches and intense pain. It can also help to alleviate aches and pains such as muscle spasms and menstrual cramps.It also aid in helping you get to sleep faster and achieve a more restful repose

2.Dark Chocolate

Dark chocolate in the diet can reduce stress in two ways via its chemical impact and its emotional impact.Chocolate is one such universal favourite, which does not need an introduction, it’s indulgent taste and captivating aroma is enough to lift up your mood and make you happy. But recent studies shows that Dark chocolate is rich in polyphenols especially flavonoids, serotonin, theobromine and magnesium which make a positive,mood elevating effect on those that ingest it.

It reduce neuroinflammation and cell death in the brain as well as improve blood flow.When choosing dark chocolate, aim for 70 percent or more. Dark chocolate still contains added sugars and fats, so a small serving of 1 to 3 grams is appropriate.

3.Nuts and Seeds

Nuts and seeds are a rich source of Omega 3 and Omega 6 essential fatty acids, which help reduce stress. Walnuts are one of the best sources of Omega 3s.Nuts are full of nutrients, including B vitamins, along with healthy fatty acids.Almonds, pistachios, and walnuts may even help lower blood pressure levels.Full of magnesium, tryptophan, vitamin B6 and antioxidants, nuts and seeds help keep blood vessels open and relaxed during highly-stressful situations and help the body secrete serotonin.

They play a significant role in stabilizing energy as well as regulating the nervous system.Cashews and sunflower seeds boosts serotonin production and can take the edge off a stressful day.Also consider pumpkin seeds and chia seeds which boosts your energy and focus.Just remember to limit servings to just a handful a day to avoid excess calories.

4.Yogurt

Eating probiotic-rich yogurt twice a day for a month could help relieve anxiety and stress by reducing activity in the emotional region of the brain.Probiotics supports gut-brain-axis — an intricate system between the gastrointestinal tract and the brain.Also, research suggests healthy gut bacteria may be linked with better mental health.

Eating curd in the morning keeps us fit throughout the day as It lets good bacteria grow and helps increase immunity.Fermented foods like yogurt can be an important dietary source of live microorganisms.Microbes in fermented foods helps to digest, produce vitamins like B12 and K, introduce new compounds to the foods that are delivered to the gut.Yogurt works to convert stress hormones in the brain into feel-good hormones.

5.Whole Grains

Whole grains, such as brown rice, oatmeal, and whole-wheat bread, and other complex carbs, such as starchy veggies and legumes, can combat stress by releasing “happy hormones” in the brain.we need carbohydrates, especially for optimal brain function. Whole grains have been shown to reduce mood swings, depression, and anxiety.

Many whole grains contain high levels of tryptophan. This amino acid is crucial for the synthesis of serotonin, as well as melatonin.The neurotransmitter serotonin, is known to lift mood and relax the body. Melatonin is responsible for our sleep cycles, which is vital for our mental health. They are also amazing for energy, reducing fatigue. Incorporate some of the following whole grains into your daily diet; barley, buckwheat, rye, wild and brown rice, quinoa, and millet.

6.Chick Peas

Chickpeas are not only less calorific then other carbohydrates they contain high levels of fibre, protein, vitamins, minerals and other goodies for both your physical and mental health.Chickpeas provide both tryptophan and slow-burning carbs, which is a great combination for reducing stress. Chickpeas also contain a large amount of folate, which is a B vitamin that helps regulate mood.

A cup of cooked chickpeas has more than 70% of your daily recommended intake of folate. The popular legume also has high protein and fiber content, which can help steady blood sugars and stabilize your mood.Roasted chickpeas are an amazing snack for warding off anxiety because they’re rich in vitamin B6, which is necessary for your brain to manufacture serotonin.The crunch factor of the roasted chickpeas can help with your body’s stress response

7.Eggs

Eggs are often referred to as nature’s multivitamin because of their impressive nutrient profile.Egg yolks are a rich source of Vitamin D. Eggs are also an excellent source of complete protein, which implies it contains all the essential amino acids that your body needs for growth and development. Eggs also contain tryptophan, an amino acid that helps in creating serotonin. The essential neurotransmitter regulates your mood, sleep, memory and behavior.

Serotonin also improves your brain function and relieves anxiety. You can include boiled eggs in your breakfast to relieve stress.Eggs contain choline, a nutrient that’s needed for the production of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that impacts the portions of the brain responsible for regulating mood and reducing stress
